Raising a BRAT...cute one!!

By Preeti Roy 

The moment you become a parent of the first, second, third, fourth or nth number of child, it’s a dream that you are raising for yourself. Whatever may be the level of closeness, you always say, "that this one is special", and you start dreaming; dreaming about a life together, happiness that will flow with this new addition (at this moment you don’t want to think about the nitty-gritty that involve in raising a child. They demand your world)  When Soham(my first son) was born, my dreams were born, that ‘I will do this’, ‘I will do that’. One life to live and only one life to love you my child. So wanted to pour everything in my potential to give him a good life. 

When they are little they are angels. As they start to crawl- things change, when they stand- things change further, when they run- its havoc. But with each milestone a parent feels releived and happiness knows no bounds. 

Then there is beauty of language. The first words are adorable. “umm-umm” then second words... “baaaa baa” or “maa-maaa” or “daaa-daaa”. Isnt that so cute. Then comes the words by 2 and a half year old with small sentences which profess demand, control and absolute attention. As a parent, its ‘rocking’ in all literal n non-literal terms. The ultimate is when they learn to speak, converse and argue. Not to forget, that their way of analysing stuff is beyond your control. 

I use the phrases like, "in the meanwhile", " its alright/ its OKAY", “ its okay to do MISTAKES", "I like it", "you are GOOOOOOOOOOOD", "you are funny" and "forget it". And some more classics which I cannot recollect, as my ‘mother brain' has sucked up all the vital glucose needed for the healthy functioning of my grey cells. 

So now its the turn of your child to try out these statement at you or your friends n family. Believe me, there were times when Soham's usage of the above phrases have brought smiles, embarassment, pride and emotions which cannot be expressed by me. 

The other day, while he was on his Potty seat, I mentioned,"I have packed Nutella and bread for tiffin box". Soham said, "ohh ho!”, then he paused for a second and thought and said “but I wanted ummm” then he took another few seconds and said out loud “oh just forget it!” That was so blunt and rude. That’s why I call him a cute little brat. 

(Mothers especially will know that this thing called ‘tiffin box' is the most challenging thing for the day, its early in the morning and you get to listen to this) 

I Iiked the way he used the term ‘forget it’ and that too with an attitude. But at times I wonder how he must be analsying all these terms that we use in their context and then he has his own version to throw at us.   

Whatever it is, language has its own beauty. Perceived n comprehended according to one’s abilities. So my dear fellow parents or future parents, understand that reading, talking and expressing in the right way will give your child a connection for flow of emotions and expressions and comfort in the right direction in their life. 

Best wishes to us all, 

Preeti
